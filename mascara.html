Welcome in Mascara !

Mascara Province is located in western Algeria, approximately 380 km from the capital. It covers an area of 5,135 square kilometers, making it a medium-sized province in Algeria. It is bordered by Tlemcen Province to the west, Sidi Bel Abbes Province to the north, Oran and Mostaganem Provinces to the north, and Saida Province to the south

History of Mascara

The History of Mascara is a rich tapestry of events, dating back to prehistoric times with the discovery of the remains of "Tighennif Man". The province went through several historical phases, including the Numidian period when it was part of the Massyli kingdom, followed by the Roman period which saw the establishment of important cities such as Aquas Silianas and Almijara. With the advent of Islam, the local tribes embraced the new religion, and the region witnessed significant cultural development. During the Ottoman era, Mascara flourished and became an important commercial and cultural center. The province was subjected to French colonization, but its inhabitants resisted fiercely under the leadership of Emir Abdelkader. After independence, Mascara played an important role in building modern Algeria

Archaeological and Tourist Sites in Mascara

Mascara has a rich history and cultural diversity. Some of the notable Archaeological and Tourist sites include:

  • Tighennif Site: An archaeological site containing the oldest human remains in North Africa.
  • Almijara: A former Roman site that housed a military garrison and a church.
  • Emir Abdelkader's Zawiya: A historical site associated with Emir Abdelkader, where he was sworn in by the tribes.
  • The Sycamore Tree: A historical tree that witnessed the swearing-in of Emir Abdelkader.
  • Headquarters of the Armies and the Court: A historical site dating back to the Ottoman era and the reign of Emir Abdelkader.
  • Ain El Baida Mosque: A historical mosque that witnessed the swearing-in of Emir Abdelkader.
  • The Grand Mosque: A large Ottoman mosque.
  • The Wetland: A natural reserve characterized by its biodiversity.

These sites offer a glimpse into the rich history and cultural heritage of Mascara.
